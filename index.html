02
Your Role in the Pod
Your mandate: Drive post-sale strategy, protect renewals, and grow incremental and LOB revenue within existing accounts. In the H2 2026 pod model, the AE quarterbacks the account; you are the post-sale lead. You're a pod, not a relay race.
2a What you own
  • Campaign delivery — media plan execution with AdOps, creative strategy, flight scheduling, performance reporting
  • Renewals — lead the conversation and secure on-time renewals at or above current spend
  • Incremental bookings within the current buying center (add-on placements, newsletter expansion, timing extensions)
  • Post-campaign reporting — use performance data to anchor renewal and upsell conversations
  • Surface expansion signals (new LOBs, new stakeholders, budget shifts) to your AE for pursuit
  • Flag at-risk accounts and churn signals to the AE with a recommended save plan
2b AE vs. AM — who's the contact

AE is the strategic contact — exec-level conversations, pricing discussions, QBR narrative, annual planning.

You're the operational contact — campaign logistics, creative, reporting, day-to-day requests.

Never surprise your pod partner — if you hear something strategic on a call, relay it to the AE same day (and vice versa). Both of you are CC'd on shared-account emails.

2c Incremental vs. expansion — drawing the line
This is the most common source of confusion. The rule:

You own — Incremental: same buying center, same budget holder, same LOB. Adding placements to an existing campaign, extending a flight, increasing frequency, adding Quick Links or takeover days to an existing IO.

AE owns — Expansion: new buying center or LOB, new budget holder not involved in the original sale, net-new use case, or anything requiring a new discovery cycle or proposal.

Gray-area rule: If you're not sure, discuss it in your pod sync. Default to AE if it requires discovery or a new proposal; default to you if it's a natural extension of an existing commitment. Full detail in the Pod R&R Explainer.
03
Campaign Delivery & Launch
Objective: Take a closed deal from handoff to live campaign without a beat dropped. This is the lifecycle of a campaign.
3a Receiving the handoff
  • The AE completes an internal handoff brief when the IO is signed: account context, buyer personas, strategic goals, and any commitments made during the sale.
  • Review it before kickoff. Anything unclear, raise it with the AE — don't guess.
3b Kickoff & launch
  1. Kickoff call — co-led: the AE makes intros and frames strategy, you run the operational agenda. From here you own day-to-day client comms.
  2. Media plan finalization — AE approves; you own execution.
  3. Creative spec collection — you own.
  4. Inventory holds & trafficking — you own, with Ad Ops.
  5. Launch QA — you own. Verify placement, links, and tracking before the issue ships.
3c During the flight
  • Run mid-campaign performance checks. Notify the AE of any underperformance early.
  • Own day-to-day client requests; loop the AE in only for strategic issues.
04
Performance & Reporting
Objective: Turn campaign data into a story that anchors the renewal. The wrap report is the setup for the next conversation.
4a Build the report
  • Use Cowork to pull placement performance, clicks, and CTOR, and to draft the client-facing report + email.
  • Anchor every number to the client's stated KPI from the original sale — not just raw clicks.
  • See the Measuring Success with Newsletters framework.
4b Reframe last-click before they ask
  • Most buyers don't click. Push clients to also track brand-search lift, direct-traffic spikes, and sales-call mentions in the campaign window.
  • Offer the company-level click report (clicks/opens by domain) as proof of intent for ABM-leaning buyers.
  • Full playbook: Measurement Discovery + Challenging Last Click.
Click discrepancies: We track via AWS SES server-side, which can't be blocked by ad blockers; their client-side pixel can. If a client's analytics show fewer clicks, recommend a link shortener on their end — it lines up much closer to our numbers.
05
Renewals, Incrementals & Expansion
Objective: Protect and grow revenue in your book. Renew on time at or above current spend, close incrementals, and feed expansion to your AE.
5a The renewal timeline
  1. 90 days out — you own first touch. Brief the AE on your renewal strategy.
  2. Negotiation & pricing — co-owned. You lead the ops; the AE leads pricing if it's contested or a significant change.
  3. Close & IO — you own standard renewals. The AE owns it if there's a significant pricing change or churn risk.
5b At-risk & churn
  • Flag risk early with a recommended save plan. The AE leads any exec-level intervention.
  • On churn, run a joint post-mortem with the AE.
5c Incrementals vs. expansion
  • Incremental (same buying center): you close and book it.
  • Expansion (new LOB / budget holder / use case): surface the signal, the AE takes the discovery call. Pricing beyond standard renewal authority goes through the AE / Deal Desk.
Pricing posture: We lead with value. Renewal and incremental pricing follows Deal Desk policy — escalate anything outside standard authority rather than discounting on your own.
06
Quarterly Business Reviews
Objective: Make the client feel like they have a team, not a single point of contact — and set up the renewal.
6a Who does what
  • The AE leads the strategic narrative — where the account is going, the growth plan.
  • You build the deck and the performance data, and build the renewal story. You co-present.
6b Build it with Cowork
  • Use the QBR / deck-building workflow (covered in Laurel's deep dive) to assemble data and slides fast.
  • Lead with the KPI story from the wrap report, then the growth recommendation.
07
Objection Handling (Renewal & Performance)
Objective: Handle the objections that come up at renewal and in performance conversations — confidently and on-voice.
7a The ones you'll hear most
  • "The clicks didn't match our analytics." → Server-side vs. client-side tracking; recommend a link shortener (see section 04).
  • "We didn't see ROI." → Reframe beyond last-click; bring brand-search lift, direct traffic, and the company-level click report.
  • "It's too expensive to renew." → Lead with CPM efficiency vs. other channels and the performance story; escalate pricing levers through Deal Desk.
Full library with real call examples and coaching notes: Objection Handling Playbook.
08
Pipeline Hygiene & Forecast
Objective: A clean pipeline is the only honest pipeline. You own accuracy for renewal and incremental deals.
Confirm with Tyler / RevOps: The exact renewal/incremental pipeline stage names and forecast weights live in HubSpot and differ from the new-business pipeline. Drop them in here once confirmed — not estimated.
8a The renewal pipeline (shape)
  • Renewal identified (90-day horizon) → renewal outreach → renewal proposal/negotiation → IO sent → renewed (or churned).
  • Incremental bookings run alongside as their own deals within the buying center.
8b Hygiene rules — every Friday
  • Every open renewal/incremental has a future close date — no past-dated deals.
  • Every open deal has a next step + next-step date.
  • Every renewal in negotiation+ has its criteria filled out.
  • Stuck deals (no activity in 30 days) get a save plan or move to the right stage.
09
What Works (Field Wisdom)
  • Set expectations & keep your word. "I'll send the report Thursday" → send it Thursday. Reliability is the whole job post-sale.
  • Lead the renewal with the KPI, not the clicks. Tie performance back to what the client said success looked like.
  • Surface expansion early. A new team or budget holder mentioned on a delivery call is a signal — flag it to your AE that day.
  • Never let the client feel handed off. Warm intros only; the goal is to build the AE's relationship alongside yours, not replace yours.
  • Calibrate, don't over-promise. "I expect this to perform at category average — here's what that means in clicks and CTOR."
10
Performance Bar
Why it matters: What "good" looks like for an AM at TLDR. Public bar so you always know where you stand.
AM metrics: renewal rate (gross and net dollar retention), incremental bookings within existing buying centers, campaign delivery quality (on-time, spec compliance, client satisfaction), and expansion signals surfaced to the AE. Reference the leveling framework for expectations by level.
